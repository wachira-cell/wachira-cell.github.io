What Makes a Good Education?

A UNESCO Perspective

Education is often measured by grades, exams, and certificates. Yet, according to UNESCO, the true quality of education goes far beyond academic results. A good education develops the whole person and equips learners to live meaningful, responsible, and productive lives.

The Four Pillars of Education

Learning to Know

A good education helps learners understand the world around them and learn how to learn. It promotes curiosity, critical thinking, and deep understanding rather than rote memorization.

Learning to Do

Education must prepare learners to apply knowledge in real-life situations. This includes problem-solving skills, adaptability, teamwork, and the ability to respond creatively to new challenges.

Learning to Live Together

UNESCO emphasizes education that promotes peace, tolerance, and respect for diversity. Learners should develop social responsibility, dialogue skills, and respect for human rights.

Learning to Be

Beyond knowledge and skills, education should support personal development, ethics, creativity, emotional intelligence, and lifelong learning.

Research

Research remains a vital investment for sustainable growth and informed decision-making. In both education and economics, research plays a central role in shaping policies that guide effective leadership, planning, and the management of scarce educational resources. Evidence-based research ensures that policies are not driven by assumptions or political convenience, but by data, context, and long-term societal goals.
